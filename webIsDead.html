The two articles we read both proclaim that the "web is dead", but the internet lives on. It's kind of strange to think that the web
can be dead but the internet lives, as we both think that they are synonymous, but the articles are trying to make a different assertion.
The articles claim that the original idea of the web, the idea that computers can talk to other computers and link to each other via HTTP, is
dead and replaced by closed communities of people working and consuming and hardly moving between each other. Also, the introduction of
proprietary APIs have killed the idea of free open internet.

The first claim the articles make is that the web is not a flat web anymore, but rather a stratified world in which people congregate into
certain locations and never leave. Essentially, little villages have popped up in the internet that companies have made, promising information
as long as the users who visit the site don't leave and open up their personal information to them. This is apparently saddening to the writers,
who say that the original idea of the web is dead. I don't agree with this assertion. I feel like its amazing that these communities spring up.
The companies set up the communities, but being able to find the information that you want more easier makes the internet a better place. I really
believe that the old days of not being able to easily find people who think like you were not a fun place to be, because it caused internet interactions
to be very risky as you could find a person who vehemently disagreed with you. These days, with these new communities, the web is simply a more interesting,
user friendly place.
